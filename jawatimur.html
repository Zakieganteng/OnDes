Candi Jawi

Candi Jawi is a temple complex located in Candirejo Village, Pungging Subdistrict, Mojokerto Regency, East Java, Indonesia. The temple dates back to the 13th century AD and is part of a complex of Hindu-Buddhist temples scattered across East Java.

Detail

Candi Jolotundo

Jolotundo Temple is a Hindu temple site located in Candirejo Hamlet, Trawas Village, Mojokerto Regency, East Java, Indonesia. The temple is thought to date back to the 10th century AD and is known for its scenic location on the slopes of Mount Penanggungan.

Detail

Candi Kidal

Kidal Temple is a Hindu temple located in Kidal Village, Tumpang District, Malang Regency, East Java, Indonesia. The temple dates back to the 13th century AD and is thought to be part of a larger temple complex in the past. Kidal Temple is famous for its magnificent architecture and delicate relief ornaments.

Detail

Candi Penataran

Penataran Temple, also known as Panataran Temple, is a Hindu-Buddhist temple complex located in Penataran Village, Nglegok District, Blitar Regency, East Java, Indonesia. Built in the 12th century AD, Penataran Temple is one of the largest temple sites in East Java.

Detail

Candi Singosari

Singosari Temple is a Hindu temple located in Candirenggo Village, Singosari District, Malang Regency, East Java, Indonesia. Built in the 13th century AD, Singosari Temple is a relic of the Singhasari Kingdom. Although now only a few buildings remain, the temple still retains the beauty of classic East Javanese architecture.

Detail

Candi Brahu

Brahu Temple is a Hindu temple located in Trowulan, Mojokerto Regency, East Java, Indonesia. Built in the 14th century AD, Brahu Temple is part of the Trowulan archaeological complex that is considered the former capital of the Majapahit Kingdom. The temple has a unique structure, consisting of a main chamber and four supporting pavilions around it.
